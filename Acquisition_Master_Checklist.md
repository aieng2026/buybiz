# How to Buy a Business — Master Checklist

End-to-end acquisition process for an individual buyer, SBA-financed, "Buy Then Build" thesis.
Cross-references the rest of the toolkit (Scorecard workbook, broker & lender email templates, SBA pre-qual checklist).

Typical timeline: **45–90 days** from LOI to close for a sub-$5M deal (clean); longer if complex.

---

## PHASE 0 — Get Ready (Foundation) — do BEFORE hunting
- [ ] Get **SBA pre-qualified** with a PLP lender → pre-qual letter (see `SBA_PreQual_Checklist.md`)
- [ ] Confirm **proof of funds** for equity injection + post-close reserves
- [ ] **Build your deal team** (line up before you find a deal — speed kills deals):
  - [ ] M&A / transaction attorney (SMB acquisitions)
  - [ ] CPA / Quality of Earnings (QoE) provider
  - [ ] SBA PLP lender (2–3 relationships)
  - [ ] Insurance broker for the new entity
- [ ] Lock your buy box (`Buy Box` tab) and have the screening Scorecard ready

## PHASE 1 — Source & Screen
- [ ] Set marketplace alerts (BizBuySell, BizQuest, Axial) per industry × state
- [ ] Send broker intro emails to 5–8 brokers (`Buyer_Introduction_Email.md`)
- [ ] Vet brokers (`Broker Vetting` tab)
- [ ] Run every listing through the Scorecard tab → Advance / Clarify / Pass
- [ ] Begin off-market outreach (CPAs, owners 60+, industry associations)

## PHASE 2 — First Contact & Seller Call
- [ ] Sign NDA → request CIM / financials
- [ ] Ask the killer questions:
  - [ ] "If you disappeared for 6 months, what breaks?" (key-person risk)
  - [ ] "Who runs day-to-day without you?" (real management in place?)
  - [ ] "% of revenue under contract / recurring?"
  - [ ] "Why selling, and why now?" (real reason for sale)
  - [ ] "Open to a seller note + 6–12 month transition?"
- [ ] Run industry-specific killers (`Industry Checks` tab)
- [ ] Confirm it clears all kill switches

## PHASE 3 — Letter of Intent (LOI)
- [ ] Attorney reviews/drafts LOI
- [ ] Lock key terms: price, structure (asset vs. stock), seller note on FULL STANDBY, transition period, exclusivity (60–90 days), escrow
- [ ] Secure exclusivity (no-shop) during due diligence
- [ ] Notify lender → begin full SBA package

## PHASE 4 — Due Diligence (make-or-break, 30–90 days)

### Financial — the #1 workstream
- [ ] Quality of Earnings (QoE) report — NON-NEGOTIABLE (2–4 wks, $25–100K)
- [ ] 3 yrs tax returns + financials reconciled to bank statements
- [ ] Customer concentration analysis (QoE often finds top-1/top-5 higher than disclosed)
- [ ] Verify add-backs / true EBITDA
- [ ] Define "normal" working capital level (avoid the 90-day surprise)
- [ ] Sales-tax exposure check (found in 25–40% of lower-middle-market deals; 5–15% of revenue)

### Legal
- [ ] All contracts reviewed for no-assignment / change-of-control clauses
- [ ] UCC lien search; litigation search
- [ ] Lease assignability + remaining term
- [ ] Licenses/permits transferable, in business's name
- [ ] IP ownership confirmed

### Operational
- [ ] Interview the GM and #2s WITHOUT the owner present
- [ ] Confirm management stays post-close (retention agreements?)
- [ ] Process documentation exists (note what lives only in people's heads)
- [ ] Systems / software review
- [ ] Mystery-shop the business

### HR / Employees
- [ ] Employee roster, comp, tenure, classifications (W-2 vs 1099 — verify no misclassification)
- [ ] Key-employee retention / non-competes
- [ ] Identify owner-relatives on payroll

### Commercial
- [ ] Validate revenue sustainability (competitors, market, customer behavior)
- [ ] Confirm no major customer about to churn / competitor opening nearby

> Findings don't kill deals — they get priced. Concentration → earnout; tax exposure → escrow/indemnification; transition gaps → longer transition services agreement.

## PHASE 5 — Financing (run parallel to DD)
- [ ] Submit full SBA package (SBA Form 1919, financials, projections)
- [ ] Share QoE with lender
- [ ] Appraisal / business valuation ordered
- [ ] Confirm DSCR >= 1.25x holds on final numbers
- [ ] Receive commitment letter
- [ ] Verify seller note structured on FULL STANDBY (so it counts toward equity)

## PHASE 6 — Definitive Purchase Agreement & Closing
- [ ] Attorney drafts Asset Purchase Agreement (APA) — asset deals typical/preferred for SBA
- [ ] DD findings → reps & warranties; indemnification escrow (5–10%, held 12–18 mo)
- [ ] Non-compete (5 yrs, geography-specific) locked
- [ ] Transition services agreement (6–12 mo, performance-tied to retention)
- [ ] Working capital peg finalized
- [ ] New entity formed; bank accounts, EIN, insurance, payroll set up
- [ ] Contracts/licenses/lease assignment consents obtained
- [ ] Closing → funds disbursed, keys transferred

## PHASE 7 — Transition & First 90 Days (the "Build" begins)
- [ ] Month 1: change nothing — observe, document, build trust
- [ ] Map every process living in someone's head
- [ ] Meet every key customer & employee (with, then without, the seller)
- [ ] Keep the seller engaged per transition agreement (avoid undermining)
- [ ] Identify the 1–2 systems driving 80% of efficiency gains (your ops edge)
- [ ] Stabilize, THEN optimize

---

## The Non-Negotiables (don't close without these)
1. QoE report
2. Indemnification escrow
3. Seller note on full standby
4. Non-compete
5. Transition period, performance-tied
6. Management verified independent of the owner
