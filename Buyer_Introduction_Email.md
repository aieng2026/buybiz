# Buyer Introduction — Broker Outreach Templates

> Purpose: get on a broker's A-list as a *qualified, specific, serious* buyer.
> Brokers prioritize buyers who are easy to transact with. This email signals exactly that.

---

## VERSION 1 — Cold email to a broker (general)

**Subject:** Qualified buyer — SBA-financed, $600K+ EBITDA, [INDUSTRY] in [STATE]

Hi [Broker name],

I'm an individual acquirer actively looking to buy and operate one established business, and your firm came up as a credible advisor in [state/industry]. I wanted to introduce myself directly so I'm on your radar when a fitting listing comes in.

**My buy box:**
- **Profit:** $600K+ in EBITDA/SDE
- **Deal size:** up to $10M
- **Financing:** SBA (pre-qualification in progress) — ~10% equity, ready to move
- **Industries:** [e.g., commercial cleaning, IT managed services, commercial landscaping, self-storage, RV/boat storage, car wash]
- **Geography:** FL, NJ, NY, PA, CT
- **Profile:** recurring/contract revenue, management team in place, owner retiring
- **My background:** A career operator across agriculture, equipment, hospitality, education, distribution, and services — I've run a family dairy/cattle and cheese operation, a heavy-equipment rental and operating business, a restaurant, a technical-skills training institute (programming, graphic design, administration), a consumer-goods distribution business covering ~25% of the country, and a printing/copy services company. That breadth is exactly why I'm comfortable in any industry: I optimize operations, not a single trade. I'm buying to operate and grow, not to flip.

I'm a serious, prepared buyer: I move quickly on the right deal, I'm working with an SBA lender and an M&A attorney, and I respect a clean process. If you have anything current that fits — or that's coming to market soon — I'd welcome a conversation.

Could we set up 15 minutes this week?

Best,
[Your name]
[Phone] · [Email] · [LinkedIn]

## Operating Background (pull from as needed)

Use the short paragraph in the emails above for outreach. Use this fuller version for a buyer profile one-pager or when a broker asks "tell me about yourself" on a call. Each line is an industry you've operated in — lead with the ones closest to the deal at hand.

- **Agriculture & food production** — family dairy and cattle operation; milk, cheese production
- **Equipment rental & operations** — heavy-equipment rental and operating business
- **Hospitality / F&B** — owned and ran a Spanish restaurant
- **Education / vocational training** — founded/ran a technical-skills institute (programming, graphic design, administration)
- **Distribution & multi-region sales** — consumer-goods (soap) distribution covering ~25% of the country's states
- **Print & business services** — printing and copy business

**Why it matters to a seller/broker:** This breadth proves you can step into an unfamiliar industry and run it — you manage operations, logistics, people, and margins, not a single trade. Pair it with your optimization focus: *"I buy businesses with a solid foundation and make them run better."*

**Positioning by target industry — which experience to emphasize:**
| Deal industry | Lead with |
|---|---|
| Commercial cleaning / landscaping | Distribution/logistics + equipment ops (routes, crews, assets) |
| IT managed services | Tech training institute + administrative/operations |
| Self-storage / RV-boat storage | Equipment rental (asset utilization, real-estate-backed ops) |
| Car wash | Multi-region distribution + restaurant (throughput, recurring/retail customers) |

## Tips
- **Personalize the first line** of Version 1 — reference a specific listing of theirs or their specialty. Generic blasts get ignored.
- **Lead with "qualified" + "SBA-ready."** Brokers screen out tire-kickers; prove you're not one in the first two lines.
- **Keep it short.** A broker reads this on their phone between showings.
- **Have your proof ready** for the call: proof of funds for the equity injection, SBA pre-qual letter, attorney name. Mentioning them converts you from "a buyer" to "the buyer."
- **Send to 5–8 brokers** per the sourcing map. Treat replies as the start of relationships, not transactions.
