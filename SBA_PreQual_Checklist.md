# SBA Pre-Qualification Checklist

> Goal: get a written pre-qualification letter from an SBA **Preferred Lender (PLP)** based on your personal profile — *before* you have a target. It makes you a credible, fast, "funded" buyer.
> Eligibility confirmed: U.S. citizen ✅  |  SBA 7(a) + 504 combined limit up to $10M (eff. July 4, 2026)

---

## Phase 1 — Eligibility (confirm settled)
- [x] U.S. citizen or lawful permanent resident
- [ ] No unpaid federal debt (tax liens, defaulted federal loans = disqualifying)
- [ ] Target industries are SBA-eligible (all six on shortlist qualify; storage qualifies as owner-operated)

## Phase 2 — Personal Document Packet (the pre-qual runs on these)
- [ ] 3 years personal tax returns
- [ ] Personal Financial Statement — **SBA Form 413**
- [ ] Credit report (pull your own first; target 680+; fix what's fixable)
- [ ] Proof of liquid funds for equity injection (bank / brokerage statements)
- [ ] Operating-background resume/bio (frame multi-industry record as management experience)
- [ ] Citizenship proof (passport)

## Phase 3 — Deal-Specific Package (after you have a target + LOI)
- [ ] Signed LOI
- [ ] **SBA Form 1919** (Borrower Information)
- [ ] Target: 3 years business tax returns + interim financials
- [ ] Target: P&L, balance sheet, debt schedule
- [ ] Your projections + business plan (grounded in target actuals — no hockey sticks)

---

## What Lenders Evaluate
| Factor | Bar | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Credit | 680+ FICO | No SBA-mandated minimum above $350K (as of Mar 2026); pattern > score |
| Liquidity | Injection + reserves | Don't be cash-zero at close |
| Management experience | Relevant industry OR general operating | **Your strength** — multi-industry operating record |
| Target cash flow | DSCR ≥ 1.25x (1.5x preferred) | Deal-specific |
| Equity injection | ≥ 10% of project cost | Up to half can be a seller note on full standby |

## Equity Math
| Deal type | Deal size | 10% equity | Cash if 5% + 5% seller note (full standby) |
|---|---|---|---|
| Cleaning / MSP / Landscaping (7a) | $2.4–3.6M | $240–360K | **$120–180K cash** |
| Car wash (7a/504) | $4–5M | $400–500K | ~$200–250K cash |
| Self / RV storage (504) | $8–9M | $800–900K | 504 down-payment rules differ; more cash |

*Keep post-close reserves beyond the injection.*

---

## Process & Timeline
1. Confirm eligibility — done
2. Assemble personal docs + pull credit — week 1
3. **Pre-qualify with a PLP lender** — 1–7 days → pre-qual letter + deal-size range
4. Use letter in broker outreach
5. Find target → run scorecard → sign LOI
6. Submit full package (1919 + financials) at LOI
7. Underwriting / QoE / appraisal / SBA approval — **30–45 days (PLP)** vs 60–90 (non-PLP)
8. Close & fund — total 45–90 days from application

## Choosing a Lender — Insist on PLP
- PLP lenders approve **in-house** (no SBA pre-review) → 30–45 days vs 60–90.
- Find via **SBA Lender Match** (sba.gov) + referrals from brokers/CPAs/attorneys.
- Active SBA acquisition lenders to consider: Live Oak, Huntington, Byline, and other PLP specialists.
- **Talk to 2–3** — appetite by industry, terms, and speed vary. Pick one that's closed deals in your target verticals.

## Pitfalls to Avoid
1. Starting at your local non-PLP bank (slow).
2. Being cash-zero at close — budget injection **+** reserves.
3. Unsupported projections — ground them in target actuals.
4. Seller note not on **full standby** — only then does it count toward equity. Lock it in the LOI.
5. Waiting for a deal to start — pre-qualify first.
